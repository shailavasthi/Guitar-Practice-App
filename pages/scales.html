Scales

The general steps to learning a new scale pattern are as follows: First, drill the pattern into your muscle memory by playing it up and down in various patterns. Next, begin crafting your own musical lines within the pattern. Finally, since the end goal of learning scales is to solo over chord progressions, use the new pattern to solo over a progression in the appropriate key. You can do this by playing along to songs, recording a progression on a looper pedal, or looking up backing tracks on YouTube

Pentatonic Scales

For Beginner Players

Learning the pentatonic positions should be a beginner player's first goal in learning how to solo.

Modes

For Intermediate Players

Once a player can solo over common chord progressions using all five pentatonic positions connected together, they should begin learning the modes.

Chord Tones

For Advanced Players

When a player has can solo over any chord progession using the appropriate major scale and all of its modes joined together accross the fretboard, this player should begin studying the use of chord tones. Their soloing skills will be greatly enhanced when they can learn how to target various chord tones in their solo based on what chord in the progression they are playing over.
